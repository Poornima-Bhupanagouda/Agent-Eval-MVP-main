# Employee Leave Policy

## Paid Time Off (PTO)

All full-time employees accrue PTO based on years of service:

- **0-2 years**: 15 days per year (1.25 days/month)
- **3-5 years**: 20 days per year (1.67 days/month)
- **6-10 years**: 25 days per year (2.08 days/month)
- **10+ years**: 30 days per year (2.5 days/month)

PTO may be carried over up to a maximum of 5 unused days into the next calendar year. Any days beyond the 5-day carryover limit will be forfeited on January 1st.

## Sick Leave

Employees receive 10 sick days per year, separate from PTO. Sick leave does not carry over. A doctor's note is required for absences of 3 or more consecutive days.

## Parental Leave

- **Primary caregiver**: 16 weeks paid leave
- **Secondary caregiver**: 4 weeks paid leave
- Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of birth or adoption
- Employees may request a flexible return schedule for up to 4 weeks after returning

## Bereavement Leave

- **Immediate family** (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 days paid
- **Extended family** (grandparent, in-law, aunt, uncle): 3 days paid

## Jury Duty

Employees will receive full pay during jury duty service. Provide a copy of the jury summons to your manager and HR at least 2 weeks before the service date.

## Holiday Schedule

The company observes 11 paid holidays per year:
1. New Year's Day
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day
3. Presidents' Day
4. Memorial Day
5. Independence Day (July 4th)
6. Labor Day
7. Columbus Day
8. Veterans Day
9. Thanksgiving Day
10. Day after Thanksgiving
11. Christmas Day

## Leave Request Process

1. Submit leave requests through the HR portal at least 2 weeks in advance
2. Manager approval is required for all PTO requests
3. Sick leave does not require advance notice but must be reported by 9:00 AM on the day of absence
4. Requests during blackout periods (year-end close, product launches) may be denied

## Unpaid Leave

Employees who have exhausted all PTO and sick days may request unpaid leave for up to 30 days per year, subject to manager and HR approval. Benefits continue during unpaid leave of 14 days or fewer.
